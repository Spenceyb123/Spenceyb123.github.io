I have recently made a career change into Web Design and Development, and am excited at what I have learned, and excited to lean more!

Initially, I earned a B.S. in Communications from the University of Utah, mostly pursing it because it was a very general degree and I wasn't sure what career I wanted to pursue. At that time, I knew very little about computer programming, and so it wasn't on my radar. 

When I took a long trip abroad through southeast Asia though, I met several people who had been abroad for 6 months +, which I thought was amazing, and I learned they were able to do this because they worked in the computer science field, and could work from anywhere that had WiFi. This is where my interest was seeded. 

A few SLCC Web Design and Development courses, and a lot of self-studying later, and I have found that it is something I really enjoy.  It helps sate my curiosity for constant learning, plus I feel a bit of an endorphin rush when I solve a problem.
